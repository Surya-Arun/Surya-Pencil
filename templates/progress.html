My Artistic Journey

Progress doesn’t happen in a day — it blooms slowly, through patience and passion. I started drawing when I was about 7 or 8… I don’t remember the exact year, but I still remember the feeling — the joy of creating something new.

Age 7–8

One of My very first attempts — a horse, drawn with wonder and excitement. It may look simple, but it was where everything began.

Age 9

An elephant sketch — strong yet calm. It was my first step toward observing and capturing real-life creatures.

Age 10

Drew *Suki* from *Chhota Bheem* — my first attempt at bringing a character to life. It taught me how expressions can make drawings speak.

Age 11

Sketched *Jagu* — another step forward in capturing movement and energy. I began to understand how lines could show liveliness.

Age 14

A girl lost in emotion — this was when I started exploring depth, mood, and feeling through art. Each shade carried a piece of my heart.

Now

An elephant holding Lord Ganesha — a symbol of faith, grace, and completeness. This artwork represents how far I’ve come — from a curious child to an artist who draws with soul.

✨ Growth takes time — but art, like life, is all about the journey ✨
